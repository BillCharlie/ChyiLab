英文簡介 (Biography)

Jen-Inn Chyi received the B.S. and M.S. degrees in electrical engineering from National Tsing-Hua University, Hsin-Chu, Taiwan, R.O.C., in 1982 and 1984, respectively, and the Ph.D. degree in electrical engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 1990. His Ph.D. dissertation dealt with molecular beam epitaxial (MBE) growth and characterization of InSb on GaAs.

In 1991, he joined the Department of Electrical Engineering, National Central University, Jhongli, Taiwan, R.O.C.. Since then, he has established MBE, metal-organic vapor phase epitaxy (MOVPE), and high-speed optoelectronic devices laboratories, which house growth and characterization facilities for various III-V materials and devices. He was the Director of Optical Sciences Center, NCU, from 2000 to 2007, and Dean of the College of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, NCU, from 2006 to 2011. He is currently Chair Professor of Electrical Engineering, NCU.

His research interests are in the areas of MBE and MOVPE growth of III-V semiconductors and their heterostructures for high-speed electronic and optoelectronic devices. His current research projects include MBE growth of InP-based heterojunction bipolar transistors, quantum-dot photonic devices, and MOVPE growth of GaN-based materials for ultraviolet, blue, and green emitters as well as high-power devices.

Professor Chyi has authored or co-authored over 280 journal papers and has been granted 29 patents. He received the Distinguished Young Researcher Award of the Electronic Devices and Materials Association, Taiwan, R.O.C. in 1996, the Distinguished Research Award of the National Science Council, Taiwan, R.O.C. in 2002 and 2010, the Distinguished Professor Award of the Chinese Institute of Electrical Engineering in 2004, and the Industrial Contribution Award from the Ministry of Economic Affairs, Taiwan, R.O.C. in 2008. He also serves as a Distinguished Lecturer of IEEE EDS since 2004. He has served as an Associate Editor of IEEE Photonics Technology Letters and as an Overseas Editor of Japanese Journal of Applied Physics. Dr. Chyi is a member of Phi Tau Phi, and a Fellow of IEEE and SPIE.
